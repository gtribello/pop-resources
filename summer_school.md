During the summer school you will work in teams to prepare a presentation about understanding the climate using mathematics.  At the end of the day you will give these presentations to me and to your fellow summer school participants. The presentations are __not assessed__ although I will give some feedback.

The aim of this session is for you to have an experience of using the two ways in which we can use mathematics to understand a phenomonon.  These are:

1. We can use mathematics to analyze data that is collected from experiments.
2. We can construct simplified models of physical phenomena.

You should try to include evidence that you have engaged with both of these modes of doing mathematics in your presentation. We will do [this activity](activity-1.md) in the first session of the day so you can engage with mode 1.  We will then spend the remainder of the day on mode 2 by doing [this activity](activity-2.md).

The activities we are working on are deliberately open ended. I hope you come away from the session feeling that you have the skills to design and perform an original research project even when you are not told precisely what to do.
